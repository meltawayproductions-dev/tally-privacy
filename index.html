Tally — Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 20, 2026  •  Effective date: June 20, 2026

Tally is an app for tracking a reward "currency" (points, bucks, tokens, or anything you name) for a group — a classroom, an after-school program, a club, a team, a scout den, a family, or any set of people who earn and spend rewards. This policy explains what information Tally handles, how it is used and protected, and the choices you have. It is written for everyone who uses Tally, including people who use it in schools, programs, or other settings that have their own privacy rules.

In this policy, "Tally," "we," "us," and "our" refer to Meltaway Productions, the developer of the Tally app. "You" means the person who uses the app.

The short version

  • Tally exists to run your reward program — nothing else.
  • We do not sell your data, show ads, or use your data to train AI.
  • We use no third-party advertising or analytics trackers.
  • If you don't create an account, your data stays on your device.
  • If you create an account to share or back up a project, that project's data is stored securely in our cloud database so it can sync across the people you invite.
  • You can delete your data — a single member, a whole project, or your entire account — from inside the app.

1. Two kinds of people in Tally

Tally involves two different roles, and they're treated differently:

  • Account holders / operators. These are the people who use the app — for example a coordinator, teacher, coach, or club leader. They may create an account and enter information.
  • Members you track. These are the people in your group whose rewards you're tracking (for example, students or club members). They do not use the app and do not have accounts. You, the operator, enter and control their information.

Important: When you enter information about other people (including children), you decide what to enter, and you are responsible for having the right to do so under any laws, school district rules, employer policies, or consent requirements that apply to you. For that information, Tally acts as a service provider that processes the data on your behalf and under your direction — see Section 8.

2. Information Tally handles

a) Account information

If you create an account (required only for sharing or cloud backup), we collect your email address and an encrypted authentication credential used to sign you in. We never store your password in plain text.

b) Content you create

When you set up and run a project, you may enter:

  • A project name, currency name, and category labels you choose.
  • Member details — such as first and last names, a category, an optional note, and a starting balance.
  • Transactions — awards and spends, including the amount, an optional reason, the date and time, and (for shared projects) which account made the change.

This content is the heart of your reward program. You control it and can edit or delete it.

c) Technical information

Tally does not include third-party advertising or analytics SDKs and does not build advertising profiles. When you use a shared (cloud) project, our hosting provider processes standard technical information needed to operate and secure the service — for example, network request metadata such as an IP address — for security, reliability, and abuse prevention. We do not use this to track you across other apps or websites.

3. How your information is used

We use the information above only to:

  • Provide the app's core features — recording awards and spends and keeping balances accurate.
  • Sync shared projects across the people you invite, with the correct permissions.
  • Sign you in, manage your account, and let you reset your password.
  • Keep the service secure and working, and respond to your support requests.

We do not:

  • Sell or rent your information.
  • Share it for targeted advertising.
  • Use the names, notes, or other content you enter to train AI models.
  • Use member information for any purpose other than running your program for you.

4. Where your data is stored: on your device vs. in the cloud

  • Local projects (no account). If you use Tally without signing in, your project data is stored only on your device. It is not transmitted to us.
  • Shared / cloud projects (account required). If you create a project while signed in, that project's data is stored in our cloud database (provided by Supabase, our hosting and database processor) so it can sync to the people you invite. A copy is also kept on each signed-in device for speed and offline reading.

5. How your data is protected

  • In transit: all communication between the app and our cloud is encrypted using industry-standard TLS.
  • In the cloud: data stored with our hosting provider is encrypted at rest, and access is restricted per account using database-level access rules so people can only reach projects they belong to, with the permissions they've been given.
  • On your device: data on your device is protected by your device's own security (your passcode and Face ID / Touch ID) and by Tally's optional in-app lock, which you can turn on to require Face ID, Touch ID, or your passcode to open the app. The app's local database is also encrypted on disk using Apple's Data Protection, so its contents stay protected while your device is locked.
  • Who can see member names: for a shared project, the person who owns it controls who is invited and what each person can do. Roster visibility is a separate permission, so an operator can let a helper award or spend points without giving them access to the full list of member names — useful when a project contains sensitive names and only coordinators should see them.
  • Least privilege: administrative keys that could bypass these protections are kept only on our secure servers and are never included in the app itself.

No method of storage or transmission is 100% secure, but we work to protect your information using reasonable administrative, technical, and physical safeguards.

6. Who we share information with

We share information only in these limited cases:

  • Service providers (processors). We use Supabase to host our database, authentication, and server functions. They process data on our behalf under their own security commitments and may not use it for their own purposes.
  • People you invite. If you share a cloud project, the people you invite can see that project's data according to the permissions you grant them.
  • Legal reasons. If required by law, regulation, or valid legal process, or to protect the rights, safety, and security of users or the public.
  • Business transfer. If the app or its assets are involved in a merger, acquisition, or sale, information may transfer as part of that transaction; we'll require it to remain protected under terms consistent with this policy.

We do not sell your personal information.

7. How long we keep data

We keep your information for as long as your account is active or as needed to provide the app. When you delete a member, a project, or your account, the associated data is deleted from our active systems. Residual copies may persist briefly in routine backups before being overwritten on our standard backup cycle. Local-only data is removed when you delete it on your device or remove the app.

8. Use in schools, programs, and other settings with privacy rules

Many people use Tally to track minors or other individuals in settings that have their own privacy obligations — for example, schools and districts (which may be subject to laws such as FERPA and COPPA in the U.S., or state student-privacy laws such as Washington's Student User Privacy in Education Rights Act), as well as employers, healthcare-adjacent programs, or nonprofits with their own policies.

If you use Tally in one of these settings, please understand the following:

  • You (and your organization) are the data controller. You decide what information to enter and are responsible for complying with the laws and policies that apply to you, including obtaining any required consent (for example, from a school or from parents and guardians) before entering another person's information.
  • We act as your service provider. We process member information only to provide the app to you, only under your direction, and not for any commercial purpose of our own. We do not use it for advertising, profiling, or AI training, and we do not sell it.
  • Data minimization. Please enter only the information your program actually needs. Tally does not require a member's full name — you may use first names, initials, codes, or nicknames if your rules call for that.
  • Deletion and export on request. We will delete or return member data at your direction. Account holders can delete data directly in the app, and organizations can contact us for assistance.
  • Agreements. If your organization requires a data privacy agreement before using Tally, contact us at the address below.

9. Children's privacy

Tally is intended to be used by adults (the account holders / operators). It is not directed to children, and children do not create accounts or use the app themselves. We do not knowingly collect personal information directly from children.

An operator may choose to enter information about minors as part of running their program. When that happens in a school or similar setting, that collection is done under the operator's and organization's authority and responsibility, as described in Section 8, including any consent required by COPPA, FERPA, or comparable laws. If you believe information about a child has been entered without proper authority, contact us and we will help remove it.

10. Your rights and choices

Depending on where you live, you may have rights to access, correct, export, or delete your personal information. With Tally you can:

  • Access and correct: view and edit your account email and your project content directly in the app.
  • Delete: remove an individual member, delete an entire project, or delete your whole account and its associated cloud data from within the app (Settings → Account).
  • Export: request a copy of your project data by contacting us.
  • Withdraw from the cloud: use local-only projects, or sign out, to keep data off our servers.

To make a request or ask a question about your data, contact us using Section 13. We will not discriminate against you for exercising these rights.

11. Where data is processed

Tally is operated from the United States, and information is processed and stored on servers located in the United States. If you use the app from outside the U.S., you understand that your information will be processed in the U.S.

12. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. If we make material changes, we will update the "Last updated" date above and, where appropriate, provide a more prominent notice. Significant changes affecting how member information is handled will be communicated before they take effect where feasible. Your continued use of the app after an update means you accept the revised policy.
